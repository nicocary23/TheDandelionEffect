Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about you, such as your preferences, login details, and browsing behaviour. Cookies are a standard part of how most websites work and are essential for many internet functions.

Why Do We Use Cookies?

We use cookies to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Keep you logged in to your membership account
  • Understand how you use our website (analytics)
  • Improve user experience and site performance
  • Deliver personalised content and recommendations
  • Track the effectiveness of our marketing efforts

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. Without them, you wouldn't be able to log in, access your membership, or complete transactions. These cookies are automatically placed when you visit our site.

  • Session cookies (temporary, deleted when you close your browser)
  • Authentication cookies (keep you logged in)
  • Security cookies (prevent fraud and protect your data)
  • Language preference cookies

Analytical Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand how you use our website — which pages you visit, how long you stay, and what you click on. This helps us improve the user experience. These cookies are anonymous and don't track you personally.

  • Google Analytics cookies (track website usage)

Marketing & Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns and remember your interests. They may be shared with third-party advertising partners.

  • Mailerlite cookies (track email marketing performance)
  • Stripe cookies (payment processing and fraud prevention)
  • Facebook Pixel cookies (if used for retargeting)

Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that we use to deliver parts of our website and services:

  • Stripe: Places cookies when you make a payment to ensure secure transaction processing
  • Mailerlite: Places cookies to track email campaign performance and personalise content
  • Google Analytics: Places cookies to collect anonymous data about site usage

These third parties have their own cookie policies, and we recommend reviewing them to understand how they use your data.

How to Manage Cookies

Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Accept only certain types of cookies
  • Reject all cookies (though this may affect website functionality)
  • Delete cookies from your device

For instructions on how to manage cookies in your specific browser, visit:

  • Google Chrome
  • Safari
  • Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge

Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie banner. You can accept all cookies or manage your preferences. If you reject non-essential cookies, only essential cookies required for the site to function will be placed on your device.

Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature. If enabled, we will respect this signal and limit our tracking cookies.

Cookie Duration

Cookies have different lifespans:

  • Session cookies: Deleted automatically when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: Remain on your device for a set period (typically 30 days to 2 years)

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Know what cookies we use and why
  • Consent to or reject non-essential cookies
  • Withdraw consent at any time through your browser or our cookie settings
  • Request information about cookies we've placed
